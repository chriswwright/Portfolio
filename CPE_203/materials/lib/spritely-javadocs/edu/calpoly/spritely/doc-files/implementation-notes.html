The internal class names can get a little confusing. Generally:

  • A "Window" is the public-facing class that provides animation. Internally, it acts as a controller for some kind of display.
  • A "Display" is the physical resource that shows the animation, and is internal. There are three kinds of display:
    • SpriteScreen shows a tile-based sprite animation in text mode.
    • SpriteCanvas shows a tile-based sprite animation in graphics mode.
    • GraphicsCanvas shows general graphics-based animation (not limited to tiles). It goes with GraphicsWindow.
